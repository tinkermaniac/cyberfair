LOST CURRIES OF INDIA

Every 100 years, the food of a region completely changes. Indian food is influenced by religion, cultural choices and traditions. India is a vast country and for every 100 kilometres you travel, you encounter a new culture and a new cuisine. You encounter the same ingredients used in different ways, lending an altogether different flavour to the dishes. Over many decades, dishes have either become unavailable from the daily menu of a common man; or have lost relevance because the ingredients are difficult to get by.

There are several reasons -:

 One could be the partition of India when many delicacies from our neighboring country were lost during the partition, it wasn’t just the partition of people but also the food, culture and everything.

 Another reason is the extensive preparation and time required to cook the dish.

There are several reasons -:

Food is about bringing something into the body. And to eat the same food suggests that we are both willing to bring the same thing into our bodies. People just feel closer to people who are eating the same food as they do. And then trust, cooperation, these are just consequences of feeling close to someone.”


There are several more dishes that were lost. Many Curries were lost in India because of busy schedules, quick meals, technology etc. We must restore these curries to get back the pride of our India back which has a lot of diverse cultures. The food revolution like many other revolutions in the past can be one way out to restore these curries but till then we can only think about it and try to change these curries in our own style back with the same flavour. In the spirit of knowing about traditional dishes in India, which were extravagant and a delight to the senses, we are trying to recollect the lost recipes to preserve them for coming generations.

Dishes

Shufta Kanaguchhi: Jammu and Kashmir

Here is the dish which has the exclusive and precious mushrooms as an ingredient. This dish is basically all dry fruits sweetened gracefully. Kashmiri Shufta is one of the few sweet dishes from Srinagar. This dish is paradise in a bowl. It is a thick concoction of cottage cheese, dry fruits, saffron, milk, desi ghee and morel mushrooms. This dish was the invasion by Timur in the 15th century that left a legacy of Kashmiri cuisine.

Phulkari Pulao: from Punjab

Move over lubricant-laced hearty dishes, here’s a dish prepared from four different varieties of rice. It’s called ‘Phulkari Pulao’, which is a mix of a variety of rice. This is refreshing since traditional pulao is increasingly giving way to the biryani.

Jadoh: a Dish from North-East India

Jadoh is a Khasi delicacy from Meghalaya, with rice as its basic ingredient. It’s got pork, rice, and herbs and is richly garnished with cilantro. Served simmered and savoury! Jadoh is one of the recipes from Meghalaya which is on the verge of loss.

Tit-Koh: Tamil Nadu

This is a refreshing dish with freshness of coconut water, fish sauce and honey. It has the richness of pork and caramel. Garnished and sprinkled with red chillies on top.

Lehsun ki kheer: Rajasthan

Here is an exotic sweet dish with the goodness of khoya, milk, dry fruits and, wait for it, garlic! Yes. They had a kheer made out of garlic. Absolutely sumptuous and thick, served cold and in perfection with rich desi ghee for preparation.

Saag Gosht: Haryana

A flavorful and nutritious, at the same time hearty dish. Usually served at supper parties. Particularly preferred are the boneless lamb shoulders for this dish. We heard of butter and chicken, but this is novel!

Tavsali (Goan Cucumber Cake)

This is a traditional recipe from the Goan belt and is basically a cucumber cake. The best part about it is that the cake is steamed instead of baking it. This dish was traditionally cooked with yellow cucumber
