BE KIND AND UNITE

Food has the unique ability to bring people together, regardless of their cultural, ethnic, or socioeconomic background. Food has the power to break down cultural and linguistic barriers and create a sense of unity and understanding. Regardless of where you come from or what language you speak, everyone can appreciate the taste and flavours of good food.

MISSION

Ending hunger is one of the most important global challenges of our time. There are several reasons for this:

  1. Human suffering: Hunger causes immense suffering to individuals and communities. When people do not have enough food to eat, they are more likely to suffer from malnutrition and related health problems, such as stunted growth, weakened immune systems, and increased risk of disease.
  2. Economic development:. Malnourished individuals are less productive, which can reduce economic output and slow the development of countries.
  3. Social stability: Hunger can lead to social unrest and instability. They may be more likely to participate in protests or engage in other forms of social unrest.
  4. Environmental sustainability: Addressing hunger is essential for achieving sustainable agriculture and food systems.  People  resort to over-exploiting natural resources, such as clearing forests for farmland, which can have negative consequences for the environment.

 

 There is no single solution to ending hunger, but there are various approaches :

  1. Increasing income: Providing people with more income and economic opportunities can help to reduce hunger by enabling them to purchase food and other necessities.

  1. Investing in nutrition: Investing in nutrition programs and interventions, such as food fortification and micronutrient supplementation, can help to address malnutrition and improve the health and well-being of vulnerable populations.

SDG 2: ZERO HUNGER

     ·         Zero Hunger aims to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture.

     ·         It seeks to ensure that all people, especially those who are most vulnerable, have access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food to meet their dietary needs and food preferences for an active and healthy life.

      ·          The target of this goal is to end hunger and all forms of malnutrition by 2030.

      ·          To achieve this, efforts must be made to increase agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers, particularly women, and enhance access to food, especially in rural areas.

CHALLENGES

Main challenges faced with food security in India:

1.    Poverty: Number of people living below poverty line (BPL) has increased by 27.5% according to the Census report, which need to be overcome in order to attain the desired food security in the country.

  1. Unmonitored nutrition programmes: Importance must be given on introducing and enacting well-monitored nutrition programmes.
  2. Population: Major parts of Indian population are engaged in agriculture activities, still the availability of food for all is a challenge itself due to the increase of population.
  3. Climatic Changes: Farming and agriculture have been severely affected by climatic changes over the past few years. Some faced floods while some drought.

As India is still a developing nation, poverty is one of the most prominent issues facing the country. The lack of money makes it so hard that many cannot get enough of the nutritious food they need.
