BE KIND AND UNITE

Food has the unique ability to bring people together, regardless of their cultural, ethnic, or socioeconomic background. Food has the power to break down cultural and linguistic barriers and create a sense of unity and understanding.

ZERO HUNGER

Ending hunger is one of the most important global challenges of our time for which the Sustainable Development Goal has been created, ZERO HUNGER. There are several reasons for this:
1. Human suffering: Hunger causes immense suffering to individuals and communities. When people do not have enough food to eat, they are more likely to suffer from malnutrition and related health problems,
2. Economic development: Malnourished individuals are less productive, which can reduce economic output and slow the development of countries.
3. Social stability: Hunger can lead to social unrest and instability. They may be more likely to participate in protests or engage in other forms of social unrest.

MEASURES
Every day too many people in countries across the globe struggle to feed their children a simple, nutritious meal.
• To provide food to all the needy and deprived beggars, religious places like Gurudwaras and Mandirs are organizing langars and bhandaras. These are community kitchens, which serves meals to all free of charge, regardless of religion, caste, gender, economic status, or ethnicity. We need to launch these types of communities to provide food and move a step towards zero hunger.
• Poor sanitation, leading to diarrhoea, is another major cause of child wasting and stunting. At the time of the last National family health survey, almost 40% of households were still practicing open defecation.
