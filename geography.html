STATES CLIMATIC CONDITIONS VEGETABLES AND SPICES FOUND
Jammu & Kashmir
  • It can be generally described as cool in the spring and autumn, mild in the summer and cold in the winter
  • Summer is usually mild and fairly dry.
  • Precipitation occurs throughout the year and no month is particularly dry
  • Wheat , pulses (gram peas , masur),mustard, potatoes, saffron
  • Aniseed , Asafoetida, red chilly , cardamon, black pepper, black cumin, white pepper, black cardamon.
Bihar
  • Sub tropical climate.
  • Sweltering summer temperature and frigid winters
  • Monsoon season in Bihar is usually unpredictable and erratic. It begins in mid-June and continues until the end of September.
  • Potato, cauliflower, cabbage, egg plant, tomato, onion.
  • Turmeric, mustard, Ajowan
Gujarat
  • The plains of Gujarat are very hot and dry in summer and cold and dry in winter.
  • The monsoon season lasts from June to September.
  • Onion, garlic, tomato, brinjal, potato, Okra, Cucurbits, ground nut .
  • Ginger, coriander, cumin, fennel, fenugreek, garlic, mustard.
West Bengal
  • Summers can be extremely hot and have high humidity.
  • Enough sunshine to be warm in the day
  • The cyclones cause some rainfall in the state
  • Peas, carrot, palak, methi, radish, turnip, coriander, onion, tomato, bitter gourd, capsicum, potato.
  • Ginger, chilli, turmeric, cardamom, nutmeg, white cumin seeds
Rajasthan
  • Climate of the state ranges from arid to semi-arid.
  • Receives low and variable rainfalls and thereby is prone to droughts
  • Tomato, okra, onion, peas, cucurbits
  • Cumin, coriander, pepper, chillies, cardamom, cinnamon and cloves
Goa
  • Tropical monsoon climate with hot, rainy, and cold weather seasons and dry summers
  • Cabbages, onions, potatoes, okra, guar and tondali, ,dudhi, marrow , aalo.
  • Asafoetida, turmeric, mustard seeds, coriander, cumin, dried bay leaves, and chili powder.
Madhya Pradesh
  • Subtropical climate with three distinct seasons: winter (December to February), summer (March to May) and the rainy season (June to October).
  • In winter, the mean minimum temperature is 10°C, though it can drop as low as 1°C and rise to a mean maximum temperature of 25°C.
  • Green Peas, cauliflower, okra, tomato, potato, eggplant, onion,
  • Coriander, fenugreek, mustard, garlic and chilli, ginger.
Punjab
  • Punjab has a continental monsoonal type of climate.
  • It receives rainfall from the south-west monsoon winds between the months of July and September.
  • Temperatures usually remain between 16° to 27° in autumn.
  • The minimum temperature is around 11 °C. Summer
  • Potatoes, onions, tomatoes, cauliflower, cabbage, peas, brinjal.carrot, okra, radish, bitter gourd, and onions brinjal.
  • Turmeric, pepper, mustard seeds, , ginger, garlic,cumin and coriander
Tamil Nadu
  • Tropical climate
  • In May and June, the hottest months, maximum daily temperatures in Chennai average about 100 °F (38°C), while minimum temperatures average in the low 80°F (upper 20°C)
  • Tapioca, onion, tomato, eggplant, and okra.
  • Turmeric, fenugreek, coriander, chilli, pepper, cardamom, garlic and ginger.
Manipur
  • The climate of Manipur is moderate.
  • Climate in the western part of the state is tropical whereas the rest part of the state experiences sub tropical climate with distinct summer, winter and rainy seasons.
  • The valley gets the reflection of the heat of the summer and the cold of the winter from the neighboring hills.
  • Pumpkins, various kinds of beans, gourds, brinjals.
  • Chilli, garlic, ginger and turmeric.
Assam
  • Typically 'Tropical Monsoon Rainforest Climate'
  • High levels of humidity and heavy rainfall.
  • Sweet Potato and Tapioca, jackfruit, bottle guard, snake guard, chilli pepper, black pepper.
  • Chilli, turmeric, ginger, onion, coriander, garlic,
Andhra Pradesh
  • The summer season in this state generally extends from March to June. During these months the moisture level is quite high.
  • The coastal areas have higher temperatures than the other parts of the state.
  • Tomato, Brinjal, Bhendi, and beans in addition to spices like Onion, Chillies.
  • Ginger, turmeric, coriander seeds, and tamarind
