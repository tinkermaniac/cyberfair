Authentic Flavours of India

  1. Home

Indian food is just something out of this world and always stands out of the crowd. It is definitely popular all over the globe, be it any country. From piping hot Idlis and dosas to the very rich Butter Chicken, every dish is scrumptious and extremely versatile. Indian food is not only a wonderful combination of delicate flavors and spices, it is also a symphony of different nutrients which make it healthy.

What makes Indian cuisine exceptional?

 

·       Diversity: India is a country with diverse cultures and a rich history which are both evident in Indian cuisine. Not just every state but even every home in India has its own unique cultural background. It represents a colorful blend of different cultures and traditions. You can find Idlis, Dosas, flavorful Sambhar and Rasam in the South and Nihari and Butter Chicken towards the North, along with a plethora of varieties of dishes which come in between.

 

·       Complexity: Indian dishes are not simple and require a long time to prepare. Some may even require hours of preparation beforehand which is what makes them unique. Take Nihari, for instance, it is a dish which is cooked overnight in a bunch of spices. It involves very complicated procedures such as slow-cooking the gravy to ensure that the meat is tender, juicy and pulls apart instantly. Perfecting these techniques is also an art in itself which results in lip–smacking dishes.

·       Inconsistency: Indian food definitely tastes different everywhere with a different flavor and a different taste every single time. Many Indian dishes don’t require strict measurements, but rather, the cuisine is quite versatile. This inconsistency is what makes it extremely unique and special.

 

·       Spices: The spices used in Indian cooking are not powdered. Instead, several dishes such as Biryani requires whole spices which add a lovely aroma to it. These spices, when mixed, give a distinct and unique flavor to the dish. The complicated combination of spices and herbs in Indian cuisine makes the dishes stand out. Indians don’t use the simple procedure of using pre-mixed spices. In fact, most restaurants prefer to make their own spice mixes by powdering them which adds freshness to the dish.

 

Indian food is finger-licking good because of the unique approach to flavoring and aroma. The authentic Indian curries and smooth gravy dishes are all made with fresh ingredients and a balance of aromatic earthy spices.

 

These are some of the spices and masalas which are used to enhance the intensity of flavours, add color to a dish and even create an extra exotic note:

SPICE

FLAVOUR

Cumin

(Jeera)

It is among the most popular spices of Indian cuisine which leaves a warm & fresh taste that overpowers its slight bitterness. People usually use cumin in the form of whole seeds. However, one can roast or ground these seeds too.

Cardamom

(Elaichi)

This spice has a herbal and citrusy smell and is often used in Indian Chai (tea) and desserts.

Garam Masala

Garam Masala loosely translates to “hot spice” in English. So, as the name suggests, it adds a little spice to the dish. It is a mixture of a set of different spices which is widely used in Indian cooking. It is generally added at the end of cooking to avoid burning off the flavour of the spices.

Turmeric

(Haldi)

Turmeric is used in almost every Indian dish & not only has a warm and earthy taste but also has medicinal qualities. It adds a vibrant colour to the dish as well.

Coriander

Coriander powder is an extremely healthy spice that people utilize in different forms in Indian cuisines. The seeds, as well as the leaves of coriander, are used as seasoning to a dish. It is an important spice in the garam masala and gives off a fresh and nutty taste in food. It is popular among households in India too because it calms down stomach ailments and helps with digestion.

Saffron

(Kesar)

Saffron has a sweet honey-like smell and is often used in plain milk or tea.

Asafoetida (Hing)

This spice has a truffle-like, garlicky flavor & pungent smell. Even a pinch adds depth to many Indian dishes.

Mustard Seeds

Mustard seeds add a spicy and bitter quality to many Indian dishes and are often spluttered in hot oil to bring out their flavor.

Red Chilli Powder

Dried red chillis are grounded into a powder which add heat to the dish & a beautiful red hue

Nutmeg

(Jaiphal)

It is the inner seed of mace and has a sweet taste. It adds a warm flavour to certain dishes and is usually paired with saffron.

Cloves

Cloves have an anise-like flavor with strong medicinal qualities.

Black Pepper

(Kaali mirch)

Also known as Kaali mirch , it provides a distinct flavour, spice, and aroma to the recipe.

Bay leaf

(Tej Patta)

Bay leaf also called Tej Patta means pungent leaves, and they have a pleasant aroma & taste similar to cinnamon.

Carom seeds

(Ajwain)

Ajwain looks similar to cumin seeds, but it has a powerful fragrance. It also smells like a herb and somewhat tastes like a mix of oregano and anise.

Fenugreek leaves

(Kasuri Methi)

Also kown as Kasuri Methi, this dried herb adds a unique flavour to recipes. It is used by crushing the dried leaves and adding them to the recipe just before it is fully done. It is also used as a garnish and has a bitter-sweet taste.

Cinnamon

(Daalchini)

 It has a slightly sweet flavour and is used in many traditional recipes and many drinks like hot chocolate and coffee for a pinch of spice. It adds a unique intensity to the drink and makes it more enjoyable.

Fennel

(Saunf)

Saunf is dried fennel seeds and belongs to the flavorful and aromatic parsley family. It is a spice that aids in digestion and is consumed by many people after a heavy meal.

Dried Mango Powder

(Aamchoor)

It is a fruity type of masala that is made using dried unripe mangoes. Aamchoor is added to dishes for a tangy flavour and enhanced taste. It is best to use this spice to prepare pickles, chutneys, spreads, and soups.

Ginger

(Adrak)

Ginger is an absolutely essential ingredient for most curries, and is one half of the recipe for ginger garlic paste, used in most food of India. Both ground ginger and ginger powder are used in cooking which make any dish more flavorful and enjoyable.

Star Anise

(Chakra Phul)

Anise tastes like fennel, but sharper and less floral. It is used in some preparations of Garam Masala and is a delicious frying spice, and is the key seasoning of the incredible tamarind chutney.

Curry leaves

(Kadhipatta)

Curry leaves are one of the most enigmatic Indian spices and are best used fresh to impart a pungent, citrus-like aroma.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


 

The cuisine boasts of a variety of types of Indian Curry which collectively make up a significant part of it while individually accentuating it with each new blend of flavors they bring to the table. These irresistible curries are packed full of exotic flavour and are only a handful of what Indian cooking has to offer:

·       Butter Chicken-

A traditional classic in Indian cuisine, butter chicken is the staple of Indian comfort food. The vibrant orange coloured sauce is thick, creamy and is full of spiced tomato flavour. If you’re visiting a more traditional Indian Restaurant, butter chicken also goes by the name of Murgh Makhani and pairs beautifully with a freshly baked naan.

 

·       Chana Masala-

This is a beautiful, hot and flavoursome vegetarian curry that has been lovingly spiced using garam masala, ginger, chillies, green mango (amchoor) and tomato. The word ‘Chana’ translates to chickpea and of course, the main ingredient is chickpeas.

 

·       Rajma Masala-

A hearty and healthy vegetarian option that’s bursting with nutrition and flavor, Rajma Masala’s main ingredient is kidney beans. This curry is built on a foundation of cooked onions and tomatoes and then lavishly spiced with garam masala, red chillies, turmeric, coriander and fenugreek.

 

·       Paneer Bhurji-

Paneer, originated by the Afghans in 16th Century , stands for cheese in Turkish and Persian languages. It was used as cheese topping in Turkey and Afghanistan. But the Indian Magic changed the entirety of Paneer and it is considered as a pure dairy product used for making mouth watering dishes.

Paneer in India is used to make delectable dishes like- Shahi Paneer, Tandoori Paneer Tikka, Paneer butter Masala, Paneer Lababdar and Paneer bhurji which are loved by all. Paneer bhurji, also known as Scrambled Paneer, is one of the tempting dishes made by people of North India. It is eaten with fresh onion salad, green chilli and hot chapattis, parathas, to satisfy your hunger along with the nutritional needs. Paneer Bhurji is a dish which can enlighten your taste buds.

 

·       Chicken Jalfrezi

Chicken Jalfrezi is a delightfully flavorful curry with tender, juicy chunks of chicken in a spicy tomato sauce studded with stir-fried peppers and onions. A mashup of Chinese techniques with Indian flavors, the stir-fry comes together in under thirty minutes, which makes it a perfect weeknight meal.

 

·       Malabar Matthi Curry

Malabar Matthi Curry, an authentic Kerala style curry composed of sardines and assorted veggies like shallots and okra is a perfect blend of spicy and tangy flavours and can be relished with naan, rice or even dosa! The authenticity of the curry is its unique way of cooking. It is semi- stewed  with additional tampering to pep up the rich flavor of Indian cuisine.

 

Even though there are several cuisines around the world, Indian cuisine is unique and diverse in its own way and always holds a special place in everyone’s hearts.
