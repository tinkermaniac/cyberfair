PULIHORA 

	
 
		

Ingredients 
2 cups Cooked rice 
1/2 cup Tamarind Water 
1 tablespoon Jaggery 
1/4 cup Raw Peanuts (Moongphali), roasted (with skin) 
Salt, to taste 
For Pulihora Masala (Seasoning) 
1 teaspoon Mustard seeds (Rai/ Kadugu) 
1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera) 
1 tablespoon Chana dal (Bengal Gram Dal) 
2 Dry Red Chilli 
2-3 Green Chillies, deseeded and slit length-wise 
1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi) 
1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing) 
1 sprig of Curry leaves 
Sesame (Gingelly) Oil, for cooking 
	
Recipe 
  First get all the ingredients ready. 
  Using precooked and day-old rice where the grains are separated makes the
  Pulihora come out with great textures. Heat sesame oil to a heavy-bottomed
  and when the oil gets heated up add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dry red 
  chillis, green chillis, and curry leaves and saute until the seeds start
  crackling and the chillies are roasted. 
  Add the turmeric powder, asafoetida powder, jaggery, tamarind pulp
  and salt. Cook the tamarind pulp in the seasoning for at least 4 to 5
  minutes over medium heat until the raw taste from the pulp goes away 
  and the pulp has thickened up. 
  Once the pulp is cooked, add the cooked rice and peanuts and stir to 
  combine all the ingredients well. Cover the pan and allow the Andhra 
  Style Pulihora to absorb all the flavours from the tangy masala. 
  Once done, check the salt and spices and adjust them to suit your taste.
  Finally, drizzle some more
  sesame oil over the Andhra Style Pulihora and stir to combine. 
  This adds flavour to the dish. 
  Once done, transfer to a bowl and serve.

DONDAKAYA FRY 

	
 
		


Ingredients 
250 gm Dandakaya 
1 Teaspoon Jeera (Cumin Seeds) 
3-4 Dried Red Chilli 
6-7 Curry Leaves 
1-2 Green Chillies 
1/4 Teaspoon Heeng (Asafoetida) 
1/2 Teaspoon Haldi (Turmeric Powder) 
1/2 Teaspoon Red Chilli Powder 
1/2 Teaspoon Garam Masala 
1/2 Teaspoon Dhaniya Coriander Powder 
3/4 Teaspoon Salt 
1 Teaspoon of Lemon Juice 
1 Tablespoon Oil 
Wash, rinse and drain the tindoras. 

Recipe

250 gm Tindora, chop off the ends and cut them into thin slices lengthwise.
Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Fry the cumin seeds till they start 
to splutter. Add dried red chillies, curry leaves, finely chopped green chillies
& a pinch of hing and fry. 
1 Teaspoon Jeera (Cumin Seeds),3-4 Dried Red Chilli,6-7 Curry Leaves,1-2 Green 
Chillies, 1/4 Teaspoon Hing (Asafoetida) 
Add the thinly sliced tindoras to the pan. 
Add haldi, red chilli powder, garam masala, coriander powder, salt and 
lemon juice. 
1/2 Teaspoon Haldi (Turmeric Powder),1/2 Teaspoon Red Chilli Powder,1/2 
Teaspoon Garam Masala,1/2 Teaspoon
Dhaniya Coriander Powder,3/4 Teaspoon Salt,1 Teaspoon Lemon Juice 
Stir fry the vegetables on a high-medium flame for 3-4 minutes. 
Then cover with a lid and cook on low flame,
stirring occasionally for 15-20 minutes till cooked. Tindora Fry is ready. 
Serve it hot with any main course meal
of roti or rice.
